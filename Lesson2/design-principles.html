Design Principles Examples

Hick's Principle

Rainbow Six: Siege Match Tracker, R6:Index

This website is actually the construction of one of my close friends. Its largest strength is how there's only a couple of options in terms of what a user can interact with. Due to there not being too many options, there's very little ways a user can get confused.

Fitt's Principle

W3 Schools Home Page, W3 Schools

This website is one that helps people learn how to use a wide variety of programming languages. This website is an amazing example of Fitt's law, in that none of the buttons are overly small, and thusly, very quick to be used.

Use of Whitespace Principle

Google... just google Google Search

I don't need to introduce google, but I will mention how its use of whitespace is perfect. By making the only standout color be the header for the search bar, the main function of the page is made unequivocally clear.
